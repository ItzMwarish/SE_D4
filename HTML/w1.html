A Million Chapatis for Nairobi

President William Ruto has pledged to enhance Nairobi's 'Dishi na County' school feeding program by providing a chapati-making machine capable of producing up to one million chapatis daily.
This initiative aims to support Governor Johnson Sakaja's efforts to include chapatis in the meals provided to over 300,000 children in public schools across Nairobi. The 'Dishi na County' program, launched on June 20, 2023, seeks to improve student health, academic performance, and school attendance by addressing classroom hunger. It features centralized kitchens in each of Nairobi's 17 sub-counties, preparing and distributing hot, balanced meals daily. Parents contribute a subsidized fee of Ksh5 per meal, payable through a smart wristband system known as Tap2Eat. Additionally, the national government plans to expand the school feeding program to benefit four million children across the country, up from the current 1.6 million, with a budget allocation of Ksh5 billion. These efforts underscore a commitment to ensuring that no child in Kenya attends school hungry, thereby promoting better educational outcomes and overall well-being.
